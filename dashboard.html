The Night The Archive Burned

The transmission came in at 03:17 local time, three minutes before the scheduled blackout. I had been watching the feeds from Sub-level 4, Corridor 9 when the signal dropped — not a graceful disconnect, but a hard cut, the kind that leaves static and a faint chemical smell in the memory of the system.

By the time Agent Mireille reached the terminal room, the fire had already consumed the eastern archive wall. Not the digital records — those are mirrored across eleven nodes in the R-Network — but the physical originals. The ones that could not be copied. The ones that were never meant to exist in the first place.

Someone inside knew exactly what they were burning. And they knew we would come looking.
